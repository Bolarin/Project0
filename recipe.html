The Absolute Best Banana Bread


Want to learn how to bake this decadent banana bread? Keep reading...



This banana bread recipe is my all time favorite. It has been tried and tested by many a taste bud and it scores every single time!

Having gone through a lot of recipes and variations of ingredients prior to arriving at this particular recipe—which is a combination of the best recipes I have found plus a little extra—I can say with confidence that this recipe produces the most delicious gourmet loaf of banana bread that will have your friends and family asking for more.

Now that I have your mouth watering, let’s get right into it.

Remember, we are on the topic of fats and baking, so there are two copies of the same recipe, each with a different category of fat.


Recipe (Using solid fat)

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour

  • 1 teaspoon baking soda

  • 1⁄2 teaspoon salt

  • 1⁄2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

  • 3 very ripe bananas (the riper the merrier)

  • 1⁄2 cup unsalted butter, softened to room temperature

  • 3⁄4 cup brown sugar (or one scant cup of honey)

  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature

  • 1⁄3 cup plain yogurt

  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract (better yet, try 1⁄2 teaspoon vanilla extract
    and 1⁄2 teaspoon condensed milk extract… You won’t regret it)

  • optional — 1 cup of walnuts or any nuts of your choice, toasted and chopped

  • optional — 3⁄4 cup chocolate chips


Recipe (Using liquid fat)

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour

  • 1 teaspoon baking soda

  • 1⁄2 teaspoon salt

  • 1⁄2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

  • 3 very ripe bananas (the riper the merrier)

  • 1⁄2 cup grapeseed oil, softened to room temperature

  • 3⁄4 cup brown sugar (or one scant cup of honey)

  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature

  • 1⁄3 cup plain yogurt

  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract (better yet, try 1⁄2 teaspoon vanilla extract
    and 1⁄2 teaspoon condensed milk extract… You won’t regret it)

  • optional — 1 cup of walnuts or any nuts of your choice, toasted and chopped

  • optional — 3⁄4 cup chocolate chips



Mashing Your Bananas

The easiest way to go about this is by mashing with a fork or a potato masher. But the best way to go about it (if possible) is to go a step further from mashing, by tossing the mashed bananas into a blender and giving it a pulse or two. This makes for a smoother loaf. But if you prefer your bread textured with bits of bananas, then go ahead and ditch the blender.

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (177°C). Coat a 9x5-inch loaf pan with butter, oil or nonstick spray. Set aside.

  2. Whisk the flour, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon together in a large bowl.

  3. Using a wooden spoon (or an electric mixer), beat the butter and sugar until very well combined. Add in the eggs, one at a time, and continue mixing.

    Add in the yogurt, mashed bananas, vanilla and/or condensed milk extracts and mix until well combined.

    Next, pour in the dry ingredients and mix well. Do not overmix. If using the nuts and/or chocolate chips, now gently fold them in.

  4. Spoon the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 50-60 minutes. Check by inserting a toothpick in the center of the loaf, if it comes out clean, your bread is ready.

    Remove the pan from the oven and allow the bread to cool in the pan until the pan is warm to the touch. Remove the bread from the pan and set on a wire rack to continue cooling.

  5. You can top and garnish as desired.
